# PROPERTY OPERATING SYSTEM

## FOUNDATION DRAFT 003

### Business Domain Map

This document defines the major business domains of the Property Operating System.

Each domain represents a major operational area that landlords, property managers, and real estate organizations must manage.

---

## Primary Domains

### 1. Governance Domain

Controls business structure, ownership, permissions, audit trails, and decision records.

### 2. Property Domain

Controls properties, buildings, units, lots, land, parking, storage, and physical locations.

### 3. Leasing Domain

Controls applicants, leases, renewals, move-ins, move-outs, rent terms, notices, and occupancy.

### 4. Tenant Experience Domain

Controls tenant portal, payments, maintenance requests, communications, documents, and support.

### 5. Maintenance Domain

Controls inspections, work orders, vendors, repairs, preventive maintenance, and asset lifecycle.

### 6. Financial Domain

Controls rent, deposits, expenses, invoices, reserves, ledgers, reports, taxes, and profitability.

### 7. Compliance Domain

Controls local laws, notices, lease rules, security deposit rules, fair housing, records, and deadlines.

### 8. Vendor Domain

Controls contractors, insurance, licenses, W-9s, performance, payments, and work history.

### 9. Communication Domain

Controls messages, notices, email, SMS, call logs, templates, and legal communication history.

### 10. Intelligence Domain

Controls analytics, risk scoring, recommendations, forecasting, AI assistance, and portfolio insights.

---

## Domain Rule

Every feature must belong to at least one business domain.

Every domain must connect back to the Canonical Data Model.

No feature should be designed in isolation.

---

## Platform Design Objective

The Property Operating System must support the full property business lifecycle:

Acquire
→ Prepare
→ Market
→ Lease
→ Occupy
→ Maintain
→ Collect
→ Report
→ Comply
→ Optimize
→ Scale
